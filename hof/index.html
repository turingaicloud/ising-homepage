CSE Hall of Fame

About the CSE Department

The Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) was established in 1991 and has since grown into one of the leading computer science departments in Asia. Our department is committed to excellence in teaching, research, and innovation, producing graduates who have made significant contributions to academia, industry, and society worldwide.

Our faculty members are internationally recognized researchers working at the forefront of various computing disciplines, including artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, data science, software engineering, and computer systems. The department maintains strong partnerships with industry leaders and academic institutions globally, fostering an environment of collaboration and innovation.

The Hall of Fame recognizes exceptional CSE graduates who have made outstanding contributions to the field of computer science and engineering, whether through groundbreaking research, transformative industry leadership, or significant service to the community. These distinguished alumni exemplify the excellence and impact that define our department's mission.

Hall of Fame Members

Dr. Jane Chen (Inducted: 2021)

Chief Technology Officer, Tech Innovations Ltd.

Dr. Jane Chen, a distinguished alumna of HKUST CSE, has revolutionized the field of machine learning applications in healthcare. As the founder and CTO of Tech Innovations Ltd., she has led the development of AI-powered diagnostic systems that have improved patient outcomes for millions. Her research on medical image analysis has been published in top-tier journals and has received international recognition.

Alex Liu (Inducted: 2020)

Co-founder & Chief Product Officer, CloudScale Technologies

Alex Liu, a CSE graduate, co-founded CloudScale Technologies, a leading cloud infrastructure company that serves millions of users across Asia-Pacific. Starting as a software engineer at a major tech company, Alex identified critical gaps in cloud services and built a platform that has become essential for thousands of startups and enterprises. Under his leadership, CloudScale has raised over $200 million in funding and expanded to 15 countries.

Prof. Michael Wong (Inducted: 2019)

Distinguished Professor, HKUST CSE

Prof. Michael Wong has made exceptional contributions to cybersecurity and cryptography over his three-decade career. His research on cryptographic protocols has been adopted in international standards and deployed in critical infrastructure systems worldwide. He has served as a program chair for major security conferences and has been an advisor to government agencies on cybersecurity policy.

Dr. David Zhang (Inducted: 2023)

Founder & CEO, DataFlow Systems

Dr. David Zhang, an HKUST CSE alumnus, has built DataFlow Systems into one of Asia's most successful big data analytics companies. His innovative data processing frameworks are used by Fortune 500 companies to extract insights from massive datasets. Under his leadership, the company has created thousands of jobs and contributed significantly to Hong Kong's technology ecosystem.

Call for Nominations

The HKUST CSE Hall of Fame recognizes CSE graduates who have made exceptional contributions to computer science and engineering. We welcome nominations for outstanding past graduates (at all degree levels: undergraduate, master's, and PhD) who have demonstrated excellence in one or more of the following areas:

  • Research Excellence: Groundbreaking contributions to computer science research with significant impact on the field
  • Industry Leadership: Transformative innovations and leadership in technology companies or startups
  • Academic Achievement: Outstanding contributions to computer science education and mentorship
  • Service to Community: Significant contributions to the advancement of computing for societal benefit
  • Department Support: Exceptional service and support to the HKUST CSE department and its mission

Eligibility: Nominees must be past graduates of the HKUST CSE department at any degree level (Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD). All nominees must have made significant contributions to the field of computer science and engineering. There is no restriction on nationality, location, or current affiliation.

Nomination Process: Nominations can be submitted by any member of the HKUST CSE community, including faculty, staff, students, and alumni. Each nomination should include:

  • Nominee's name and current affiliation
  • Detailed description of achievements and contributions
  • Supporting materials (publications, awards, testimonials, etc.)
  • Contact information for the nominee
  • Nominator's name and contact information

Submission: Please submit nominations via email to hof@cse.hkust.edu.hk or through our online nomination form. The Hall of Fame Committee reviews nominations annually and inducts new members based on their exceptional contributions and impact.

Deadline for 2025 Nominations: December 31, 2025

For questions or more information about the nomination process, please contact the Hall of Fame Committee at hof@cse.hkust.edu.hk.
